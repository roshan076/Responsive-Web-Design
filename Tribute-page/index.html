Ada Lovelace

The Pioneer of Computer Programming

Here's a timeline of Ada Lovalace's Life

  • 1815 - Born in London, England
  • 1833 - Meets Charles Babbage, who inspires her interest in Mathematics
  • 1842 - Writes extensive notes on Babbage's Analytical Engine, which includes the first algorithm intented to be processed by a machine, effectively making her the world's first computer programmer
  • 1843 - Publishes her translation of Luigi Menabrea's work on the Analytical Engine, adding her own comprehensive notes, which includes the algorithm and her vision of the machine's potential
  • 1852 - Passes away at the age of 36 from uterine cancer, leaving behind a legacy as a pioneering mathematician and visionary in the field of computing.
